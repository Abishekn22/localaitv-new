## 6. Clarifications to Send Back to the AI Team

These are the questions worth returning to Nagarjuna & Sameer. They **shape Phase 1**; they **do not block Phase 0**.

**C1 — RTMP fan-out: Phase 1 blocker or Phase 3? (most important — please reconcile)**
The review puts RTMP fan-out under *Phase 3 blockers* (§1.2) and as *design-only during Phase 1* (§3.3) — **but its own bandwidth math says 300 channels ≈ ~300 Mbps sustained, which a single Hostinger VPS "cannot deliver."** That makes RTMP fan-out a **Phase 1 (300-channel) blocker**, not a Phase 3 one. Please reconcile §1.2 vs §3.3 and state definitively: at what channel count does the single-VPS RTMP approach break, what is the **Phase 1** RTMP posture, and a rough Phase 1 cost for the chosen option (AWS MediaLive vs Nginx-RTMP on EC2 ASG vs Mux).

**C2 — Cloudflare at Phase 1, not just Phase 3.**
The review says Free/Pro "likely violates the AUP" for video. At what traffic/channel count does that actually break? What is the recommended **Phase 1 (300-channel)** CDN posture, and a rough Phase 1 egress cost estimate (the review only quantifies the Phase 3 peak)?

**C3 — ABR HLS ladder: does it fit in the Phase 0/1 GPU budget?**
The review recommends a 3-rung HLS ladder (360p/480p/720p) "at trivial encoding cost." Please quantify: does adding the ladder change the G1 NVENC throughput / instance-count math? The founder will decide Phase 1 inclusion based on that number.

**C4 — pg_boss at scale.**
The review flags pg_boss queue-table co-location → hot-table/autovacuum problems at 600K jobs/day, "archival cadence and partitioning unspecified." Please specify the intended archival cadence + partitioning strategy.
